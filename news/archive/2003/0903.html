Antivirus technologies woefully inadequate
New Scientist | Submitted by: Reid Fleming
"The way we fight viruses is fundamentally flawed, because viruses spread faster than antivirus patches can be distributed. By the time the antivirus software catches up, the damage has already been done, says Hewlett-Packard researcher Matthew Williamson. ... Williamson's research, due to be presented at a conference in Toronto later in September, is the first time anyone has analysed how effective this antivirus software is. ... He found that even if a signature is available from the moment a virus is released, it cannot stop the virus spreading if it propagates fast enough. 'These fast viruses are what we are getting at the moment,' Williamson says, adding that they are getting better at being quicker."
