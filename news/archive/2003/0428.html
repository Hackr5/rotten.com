Arsonists permitted to join volunteer fire departments
Chicago Tribune | Submitted by: Reid Fleming
According to Illinois state law, convicted arsonists (and other felons) are ineligible to find work as professional firefighters. But they can be volunteers: "In May 2001, for instance, five white volunteers on the Brooklyn, Ill., Fire Department were arrested by local police and federal agents. The volunteers, all of whom lived in Missouri, were charged with setting arson fires in the predominantly black village. Under state law, the volunteers, if convicted, could continue to serve if the district's trustees allowed it."
