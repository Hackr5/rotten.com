Denver Police surveilling its own citizens
Reuters | Submitted by: Reid Fleming
The ACLU has filed a lawsuit against the Denver police department on behalf of 3,200 residents, who are believed to have been the subjects of secret DPD dossiers. The citizens were selected by virtue of their having participated in peaceful civil rights demonstrations. The ACLU contends that the secret files have shared this information with federal agencies, and that they labeled some of Denver's law-abiding townspeople "criminal extremists."
